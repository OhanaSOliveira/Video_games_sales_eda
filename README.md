# Video Games Sales – Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using a dataset containing video game sales information available on Kaggle.

---

## Dataset

**Name:** Video Game Sales  
**Source:** Kaggle  

The dataset includes information on video games released up to 2016, including:  
- Name  
- Platform  
- Year of Release  
- Genre  
- Publisher  
- Sales by region (NA, EU, JP, Other)  
- Global Sales  

---

## Objectives of the Analysis

- Identify the best-selling games and publishers by region.  
- Analyze the relationship between genre and sales volume.  
- Investigate whether the number of published titles influences global sales.  
- Determine publishers’ dependency on their most profitable genres.  

---

## Key Findings

- Nintendo leads as the publisher with the highest global sales.  
- Action, Sports, and Platform are the most profitable genres.  
- There is a strong correlation between the number of published titles and total sales (r = 0.80).  
- Some publishers are highly dependent on a single genre to generate revenue.  
- Publishers with diversified portfolios tend to show greater market resilience.  

---

## Visualizations Included

- Best-selling games by region  
- Publishers with the highest sales volume (pie chart and bar chart)  
- Correlation between the number of published games and total sales  
- Most profitable genre by publisher  
- Proportion of each publisher’s main genre in total revenue  

---

## Conclusion

The analysis highlights how sales performance is influenced by both the volume of released titles and the strategic choice of genres. Publishers such as Nintendo and EA demonstrate significant dependency on specific genres, while others benefit from more diversified catalogs.
